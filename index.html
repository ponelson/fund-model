How This Drives The Model

  • When Use Portfolio Model is on, the aggregate Gross MOIC, exit profile, and deployment schedule are ignored — the deals drive everything.
  • Capital is called in each deal's Entry Year; proceeds arrive in the Exit Year.
  • Under American waterfall, the carry shown per deal reflects the deal's own waterfall (cost basis + pref + catch-up + 80/20). LP receives = proceeds − GP carry.
  • Under European waterfall, deal-level carry is not paid until fund-level ROC + pref are met; per-deal column shows the deal's contribution to terminal carry.
  • Loss-making deals receive no carry. Under modern American (with clawback), losers reduce winners' carry at fund end. Under pure American, they don't.
  • The sector tag is informational only — it doesn't change the math but is included for diligence presentation purposes.

Definitions & Methodology

  • PIC — Paid-In Capital ratio. Cumulative capital called as a fraction of total commitments. Approaches 1.0x as the fund is fully drawn.
  • DPI — Distributions to Paid-In. Cumulative cash returned to LPs, divided by paid-in. DPI ≥ 1.0x signifies the LPs have got their money back.
  • RVPI — Residual Value to Paid-In. Remaining NAV (unrealized) over paid-in. Marks to fair value; lower over time as the portfolio is harvested.
  • TVPI — Total Value to Paid-In = DPI + RVPI. The headline multiple. Equivalent to Net MOIC once the fund is fully realised (RVPI = 0).
  • J-Curve trough — the deepest negative TVPI−1.0x observed, typically in years 2–4 as fees outpace mark-ups.
  • Realized MOIC excludes unrealized fair value — a stricter test of demonstrated cash-on-cash performance.
  • Vintage benchmark figures are illustrative; in practice consult Burgiss, PitchBook, or Preqin for current quartile data by strategy & vintage year.

Model Notes & Simplifications

  • European waterfall: applied year-by-year. LP receives 100% until fund-level ROC + pref met; then GP catch-up; then 80/20 split. Pref computed on time-weighted contributed capital, compounded annually.
  • American waterfall: each deal (portfolio mode) or each vintage year (aggregate mode) runs its own waterfall when realised. Modern variant applies end-of-fund clawback so total GP carry ≤ European-equivalent ceiling; pure variant lets winners' carry stand regardless of losers.
  • Transaction fee offset: transaction fees collected as % of deployed capital, reduced by offset % which flows back as a reduction to LP-paid management fees.
  • Subscription line: delays capital calls by N months, improving net IRR via shortened LP holding period. MOIC unchanged.
  • NAV roll-forward: Unrealised value is marked to (gross MOIC of underlying remaining deals) × cost. Mark-up is recognised linearly through hold period — a simplification of fair-value methodology.
  • Portfolio mode: overrides aggregate MOIC and deployment schedule with explicit per-deal cash flows. Useful for modelling realistic dispersion and per-deal carry mechanics.
  • Clawback: when American GP receipts exceed what European would have paid (loss netting), the excess is returned to LPs and shown as a separate waterfall step.
  • Not modelled: GP personal clawback guarantees, escrow / holdback mechanics, side-letter MFN provisions, fee waiver tax structuring, §1061 holding period analysis, sub-line interest expense, currency hedging.
